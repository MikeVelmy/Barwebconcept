# Project: Lumen Bar Accra

## Project overview
Build a premium one-page landing site for a bar concept in Accra, Ghana. The goal is to sell the vibe fast, showcase menu highlights, promote events, and drive reservations. The site should feel modern, nightlife-forward, and mobile-first.

## Tech stack
Use the existing stack in the project. Do not introduce unnecessary libraries, styling systems, or UI frameworks unless required by the project setup.

## Design system
- Visual style: dark, premium, atmospheric, nightlife-inspired.
- Colors: near-black backgrounds, dark gray surfaces, off-white text, warm gold or amber accents.
- Typography: bold display font for headlines, clean sans-serif for body text.
- Layout: generous spacing, strong hierarchy, clear section separation.
- Interactions: subtle hover states, smooth scroll, simple motion only.
- Responsive behavior: mobile-first, with strong readability and large tap targets.
- Accessibility: high contrast, readable font sizes, visible focus states, and clear button labels.

## Content rules
- Copy should be short, confident, and premium.
- Use plain language that sounds polished but not overly formal.
- Primary CTA is always "Reserve a Table".
- Secondary CTA is always "Explore the Menu" or "View Menu".
- Keep section copy concise and scannable.

## Page structure
1. Header with logo, anchor nav, and reserve button.
2. Hero with headline, subhead, and two CTAs.
3. Trust strip with short social proof items.
4. Signature highlights section.
5. Featured menu preview.
6. Events preview.
7. Gallery section.
8. Location and hours section.
9. Reservation form section.
10. Footer.

## Gallery rules
- Gallery images must load fast.
- Use modern formats where possible.
- Lazy-load images below the fold.
- Do not lazy-load the hero image.
- Keep the gallery visually clean and limited to a small curated set.
- Use a lightbox for full-size viewing.

## Menu rules
- Show only a curated preview on the landing page.
- Menu should be easy to scan on mobile.
- Include item name, short description, and price.
- Group items by category.

## Booking rules
- Keep the form short.
- Ask only for name, phone, date, time, guests, and notes.
- Reserve CTA should appear in the header, hero, and booking section.

## Do not
- Do not clutter the page with too many sections.
- Do not use heavy animations.
- Do not add random pages unless requested.
- Do not change the visual direction away from premium nightlife branding.
- Do not use PDF menus or slow image-heavy patterns.

## Quality bar
The finished site should feel like a polished concept landing page, load quickly on mobile, and clearly communicate the brand, menu, events, and booking action.
